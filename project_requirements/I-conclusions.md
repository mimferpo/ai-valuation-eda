**When:** After analysis phases F–H

## Goal

Close with an evidence-based answer to the main analytical question — not hype. A stakeholder should understand your **hypothesis** without running code.

## Tasks

- [ ] Summarise **3–5 most important findings** (bullet points, plain language)
- [ ] State your **working hypothesis** on which company looks more reasonably valued:
  - MSFT / GOOGL / too close to call — with evidence
- [ ] Tie the answer directly to valuation, profitability, and scale metrics
- [ ] One thing that **surprised** you
- [ ] **2–3 recommendations** (e.g. what to monitor, what data to add, how to read ratios)
- [ ] **Limitations** — public data only, two-company scope, Yahoo vs 10-K, not investment advice
- [ ] **What you would do next** with more time (SEC filings, quarterly depth, macro rates, additional peers)

## Framing (important)

You are **not** proving a market bubble. You are comparing whether **public fundamentals** and **market multiples** make Microsoft or Google look more reasonably valued **in this dataset**.
